# Sleep & Mindset Flashcard Deck

## Subject 1: Core Problem Identification
**Front:** Name of the sleep-disrupting cycle from labeling a day "bad"?
**Back:** "The Perfectionist's Day-Closure Paralysis."

**Front:** The cognitive distortion in labeling a whole day "bad"?
**Back:** "All-or-Nothing Thinking" or "Mental Filtering."

**Front:** Why stay up late after a "bad day" psychologically?
**Back:** To subconsciously salvage it or avoid surrendering to "failure."

## Subject 2: Morning Mindset - Prevention
**Front:** What to declare upon waking to prevent the "bad day" label?
**Back:** "Today is a neutral day. My worth is not tied to its outcome."

**Front:** Replace "Productive Day" with what primary goal?
**Back:** Aim for a "Present Day," not a "Productive Day."

**Front:** The only mandatory morning planning task?
**Back:** Identify ONE "Most Important Task" (MIT). Everything else is a bonus.

## Subject 3: Daytime Reset - Segmenting
**Front:** Immediate action when the "bad day" feeling hits?
**Back:** "Anchor Point Reset": Stop, 5-min timer, physical reset (water, breath).

**Front:** Key mental reframe after a setback?
**Back:** "This is a moment of challenge, not a bad day. The story isn't over."

## Subject 4: Evening Ritual - Critical Closure
**Front:** First step of the Evening Closure Ritual?
**Back:** "Shutdown Ceremony": Physically close work, declare "The workday is complete."

**Front:** The three non-productivity review questions? (1)
**Back:** 1. "What is one thing I did today that was 'good enough'?"

**Front:** The three non-productivity review questions? (2 & 3)
**Back:** 2. "What was one challenge?" 3. "What is one thing I learned?"

**Front:** Where do unfinished tasks and anxious thoughts go?
**Back:** In the "Parking Lot for Tomorrow" notepad.

**Front:** What to declare when using the Parking Lot?
**Back:** "I acknowledge you. I will look at you tomorrow. You do not belong to tonight."

## Subject 5: Self-Compassion & Permission
**Front:** Two statements to end the Closure Ritual? (1)
**Back:** One forced gratitude statement (e.g., "grateful for a warm bed").

**Front:** Two statements to end the Closure Ritual? (2)
**Back:** One self-compassion statement: "I did what I could. That is enough. I permit myself to rest."

## Subject 6: Pre-Sleep Rules
**Front:** Rule after the Closure Ritual regarding tasks?
**Back:** NO MORE TASK COMPLETION. Goal is disengagement, not accomplishment.

**Front:** Pre-sleep activity shift?
**Back:** From *active consumption* (scrolling) to *passive reception* (music, audiobook).

**Front:** Final mantra in bed?
**Back:** "The day is complete. I release it. My only task now is to rest."

## Subject 7: Key Mindset Shifts
**Front:** Shift FROM: "I must finish plans for a good day." TO?
**Back:** "I engage with intention and end with compassion, regardless of completion."

**Front:** Shift FROM: "A bad start ruins the day." TO?
**Back:** "A day holds many moments. Each moment is a new chance."

**Front:** Shift FROM: "Sleep is surrender to failure." TO?
**Back:** "Sleep is a necessary gift I give to tomorrow's self."

**Front:** Shift FROM: "Ambition demands constant doing." TO?
**Back:** "Wisdom knows strategic rest is part of high performance."
